5-Element Framework
How to Forecast
```
01
Past Performance
  • Pull daily, weekly, monthly historical data
  • What has this team produced under similar conditions?
  • Use leaderboards as the baseline
  • All things being equal — this is your starting number
02
Leadership Intuition
  • What state of mind are they in?
  • Are they good at home?
  • Do they understand the mission?
  • Do they have a plan?
  • Do they know their strengths and weaknesses?
  • Who is working well with each other?
03
Actual Opportunities
  • Appointments currently on the calendar
  • This is your hard floor
  • Layer the other 4 elements on top
  • Don't forecast below your current pipeline
04
Who's in the Field?
  • Who is on vacation or sick?
  • Who is struggling right now?
  • Who has time constraints?
  • Same field time as last week or less?
05
Incentives
  • What company incentives are active?
  • What can you run from your budget?
  • What gets your team genuinely excited?
  • Incentives move behavior — factor into upside
Most of these factors experienced leaders already process intuitively. Codifying them makes the process duplicable. Check these boxes weekly — that habit becomes a system, and that system becomes your edge.

Non-Negotiables
Code of Conduct
```
Respect is Mandatory
  • Treat every rep, manager, and leader with dignity — in person, on calls, in group chats
  • Disagreements go through proper channels — never in front of the team
  • Protect the unity and trust of the organization
Communication is Intentional
  • What you say, how you say it, and when you say it matters
  • Be clear, direct, and professional in all team interactions
  • Stay active and responsive in all team chats
Protect the Standard
  • You are a guardian of MVMT culture
  • Refuse to participate in or tolerate gossip or negativity
  • No public undermining — of reps, co-managers, or leadership. Ever.
You Are Always Being Watched
  • How you treat people when no one is watching is your real character
  • Your attitude, work ethic, and respect sets the tone for the entire team
  • Lead accordingly — every day, every interaction
"How you treat people when no one is watching is your real character."
